Escaping from my grandfather

My father died …… in a figurative sense. He left me when I was 4. My mother works in a public authority. Unfortunately, I wish to be alone, but I wasn’t. I lived with my grandparents. My grandmother was kind among angels, but my grandfather was evil, like the devil. He was an alcoholic. As a child, I was always afraid of him. He could start drinking at any time, and when I was alone at home, he might beat or punish me for no reason, even for something as simple as watching TV. I was too young and too weak to protect myself; I hadn’t even finished second grade. Summers were the worst because I had to spend the whole day at home, feeling unsafe. I bless my grandmother, who became my bodyguard for my grandpa. She took care of the house, cooked for me, and helped with my homework. She was something. I am deeply grateful to her.

I was a quiet and isolated child. Most of the time, I stayed alone, building with Lego, drawing, or going swimming. I often tried to stay outside just to avoid being at home. One day, my grandmother introduced me to some boys outside. I found out that the boy was with some other guy with football boots. At that time, I wasn’t prepared to play football. I had to play football to meet this guy close to me. At first, it was hard to play with sandals, but I got used to them with some injuries. My friends expanded like my skills and my inventory. I was allowed to play football on a bigger court with real goalposts instead of rocks or sticks.

In my first real match, I felt like an outsider; it wasn't as easy as my outside courts, because I had to play with some other skilled guys who threw this pass like me. Did you remember that guy with boots? He played beautifully, like a dancer who passed throws 5 guys easily, he was an identity on this court. I decided I wanted to be like him. My dribbling wasn’t great, but I was good at shooting; every last touch for me was a goal, so I decided to practise my shot. I woke up early to shoot in front of the gate until the match began at night. Every day, I practised not to escape from my grandfather, but I was engulfed. When I was on the field, I forgot everything else. Even when I broke my arm, I kept playing. My shot was improved as my friends became adults, and I got the chance to play with some former professional football players. You may not believe it, but he was my assistant, and I always scored a goal when he gave me that hope. My football career has started.

Then everything changed. My mother had to move to a bigger city. This was my chance to escape my grandfather, but it also meant leaving football behind. I had a choice: stay in my old life or start a new one. I chose to leave my past life. I decided to prepare for the entrance exams to a new school, even though studying was very hard for me. I didn’t understand many basic concepts at first, not even simple ideas in mathematics. But I kept going, solving problems again and again until I finally got the gist of it. I spent months studying, sacrificing football, while still living in fear at home. When exam day came, I was nervous. The questions looked unfamiliar, but I tried my best, refusing to leave anything blank. After the exam, I returned to football, unsure of what would happen.

Then my mother received a phone call: “Congratulations! Your son has been accepted into the National School of Physics and Mathematics.”
